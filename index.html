About

The Abdus Salam International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP) and BRAC University are excited to host the Jamal Nazrul Islam (JNI) Memorial Winter School. The JNI Winter School is one of the first of its kind events in Bangladesh which aims to bring world class experts, researchers, and innovators from around the globe to teach local students and faculty about state-of-the-art research in theoretical physics and applied mathematics. The goal is to expose students attending the program to the latest techniques and ideas so that they can help Bangladesh become a leader in these fields.

Themed "Exploring the mathematics of the universe", the school will provide a platform for applying cutting-edge techniques to the most fundamental questions such as: How did the big bang begin? What is the ultimate fate of the universe? Is information lost in a black hole? How can current quantum gravity theories like string theory be used to understand black holes and the big bang?

Duration: Preschool: January 19 – January 23, 2025, Main-school: January 28 – February 03, 2025

Location: Residential Campus, BRAC University, Savar, Bangladesh

About Professor Jamal Nazrul Islam

Professor Islam was a pioneer in mathematics and theoretical physics in Bangladesh. Motivated by the dream of creating a mathematics and physics community within Bangladesh, he left his professorship in the UK to establish a mathematics and physical sciences center at the University of Chittagong called the Research Center in Mathematical & Physical Sciences. He is an intellectual parent of the current community of mathematical and theoretical physicists, received the Ekushey Padak Award in 2001, and was a classmate and collaborator of celebrated physicist Professor Stephen Hawking. His primary field of research was early universe physics, black holes, and quantum field theory.
